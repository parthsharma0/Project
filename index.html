History

Founded in 1209, Cambridge is the second-oldest university in the English-speaking world. It was formed by scholars who left the University of Oxford after a dispute.


Location

The university is located in Cambridge, England, about 50 miles north of London. It is made up of numerous colleges spread throughout the city. The university has excellent facilities, including libraries, laboratories, and sports facilities. Its libraries, such as the Cambridge University Library, are renowned for their extensive collections.


Alumini

Cambridge has a distinguished list of alumni, including many notable figures in various fields, such as politics, science, literature, and the arts. Cambridge is a major center for research, with a focus on areas such as science, technology, medicine, and the humanities. The university has numerous research institutes and centers


Courses

The university offers a wide range of undergraduate and postgraduate courses in various fields, including arts, sciences, humanities, and engineering.
